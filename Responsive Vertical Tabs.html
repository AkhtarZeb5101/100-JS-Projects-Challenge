African Elephants

Overview

African elephants are the world's largest land animals. The two species are the African bush elephant and the smaller African forest elephant.

Key Characteristics

  • Weight: Up to 13,000 pounds
  • Height: Up to 13 feet at the shoulder
  • Lifespan: 60-70 years
  • Habitat: Savannas, forests, and deserts

Interesting Facts

  • Their trunks contain about 40,000 muscles
  • They're excellent swimmers
  • Females live in family groups led by a matriarch
  • They can communicate through vibrations in the ground

Bengal Tigers

Overview

The Bengal tiger is one of the most iconic big cats, known for its orange coat with black stripes and white underparts.

Key Characteristics

  • Weight: 400-700 pounds
  • Length: Up to 11 feet
  • Lifespan: 20-25 years
  • Habitat: Tropical forests, grasslands, and mangrove swamps

Interesting Facts

  • Each tiger has a unique stripe pattern
  • They can leap up to 20 feet in length
  • Tigers are excellent swimmers
  • They can consume up to 88 pounds of meat in one sitting

Giant Pandas

Overview

Giant pandas are beloved worldwide for their distinctive black-and-white coloring and gentle demeanor.

Key Characteristics

  • Weight: 150-300 pounds
  • Height: 2-3 feet when on all fours
  • Lifespan: 20-30 years
  • Habitat: Temperate broadleaf and mixed forests in Southwest China

Interesting Facts

  • They eat bamboo for 12-16 hours per day
  • A newborn panda is 1/900th the size of its mother
  • They have a unique "thumb" which is actually a modified wrist bone
  • They can climb trees and are good swimmers

Gray Wolves

Overview

Gray wolves are apex predators and one of the world's most well-researched animals, known for their complex social structures.

Key Characteristics

  • Weight: 70-145 pounds
  • Length: 4.5-6.5 feet
  • Lifespan: 12-14 years in the wild
  • Habitat: Forests, tundra, mountains, and grasslands

Mountain Gorillas

Overview

Mountain gorillas are one of the most majestic and endangered primates, sharing 98% of their DNA with humans.

Key Characteristics

  • Weight: 300-400 pounds (males)
  • Height: Up to 6 feet when standing
  • Lifespan: 35-40 years
  • Habitat: Mountain forests of central Africa
